### Coherence Assessment

**Dependencies (critical path):**
```
Protein 160g/day
     ↓ (muscle preservation + satiety)
Weight 95 kg  ←────── 3 runs/week
     ↓                      ↓
VO2max ~48 at 95 kg    Parc Beaumonts 12 km
     └──────────────────────┘
                ↓
         Semi < 1:45
         (global objective)
```

**Conflicts:**
1. **Protein vs. caloric deficit**: 160g protein at 20–25% fat and moderate carbs = ~1,800–2,000 kcal target. At 103 kg and ~10h/week total activity, TDEE is ~2,600–2,800. A 600–800 kcal deficit is aggressive but not extreme. No fundamental conflict — high protein facilitates weight loss while preserving muscle. Need conscious tracking.

2. **3 runs/week vs. tissue tolerance**: At 103 kg, each running stride generates ~3× bodyweight ground reaction force (~310 kg per step). Tendons adapt 2-3× slower than cardiovascular fitness. A 3x/week run frequency at this weight must be on soft surfaces and with strict rest-day separation. Premature escalation is the #1 injury risk.

3. **VO2max expectation vs. weight reality**: Garmin VO2max will continue rising mechanically as weight drops (same absolute engine, lower denominator). Benjamin should not be frustrated that his VO2max isn't yet at 45+ — it will get there automatically with -8 kg.

**Highest-leverage single action**: **Close the protein gap.** Adding 30–35g protein/day (one shake, or 150g chicken breast) would:
- Preserve the 72.6 kg muscle mass during caloric deficit
- Improve run recovery (protein synthesis window)
- Increase satiety → reduced total calorie intake → accelerate fat loss
- Unlock better ACWR tolerance → more training → more fat loss → weight to 95 kg → VO2max rise

This is the cheapest, lowest-risk intervention with the highest cross-objective cascade.
